Do I Show or Hide My Support Text?

JUNE 29, 2024

What do I mean by support text?

If it were a verbal explanation, it would sound something like this:
Person one asks, "What's your favourite restaurant in the city?"
Person two responds by saying, "Gusto. It's an Italian spot near King that has tasty food and a great vibe."

"Gusto." would be the primary response. The support text is, "It's an Italian spot near King that has tasty food and a great vibe."
In the UI UX sense here's what this looks like: [show img with single row, primary secondary - HIGHLIGHT SECONDARY]

Why am I thinking about my support text so hard?

When we talk about screen real estate, we often consider how much content we need in order communicate something effectively. The question I always ask is, "What is the minimum amount of words that we need here to explain this thing?"

Shortest isn't always the best option, but it's a good place to start because if it's concise and it's intelligible then you're winning.

Length isn't the only thing to consider when it comes to text content on your pages. There are consequences to every little detail when it comes to what content appears, and how that content is formatted, in the viewport on page load.

Most times the question that naturally comes next is, "Do we even need this content at all?"

This question is deceptively complex. Content is almost always the one thing that everyone has an opinion on, so it always results in this tug of war between what it should say, how long it should be, and whether we even need it at all - or said otherwise, "Does the primary text here do a good enough job of explaining what this thing is?"
